Adrift in the Void

Day... 210? Maybe 211.

It’s hard to tell anymore. Time has stopped being something I measure by clocks. I sleep when exhaustion takes me, I eat when hunger gnaws, and I drift through this infinite abyss with no destination, no contact, no end.

The ship's systems are still running—barely. The emergency power flickers now and then, a cruel reminder that even the machines are struggling to survive. The distress beacon pulses into the void, sending a signal to nowhere. I don't know if anyone is listening. I don't know if anyone will ever listen.

Memory Fragments

The silence is deafening. I find myself speaking aloud, just to break it, just to feel something human in this lifeless vacuum.

I replay old logs, messages from Earth, long since exhausted. They’re ghosts now. Echoes of a world that feels more like a dream than a place I once belonged to. My mind drifts as much as the ship. I remember home, the ocean waves crashing against the shore, the laughter of friends, the scent of rain-soaked streets.

But was that real? Or just a story I keep telling myself to stay sane?
